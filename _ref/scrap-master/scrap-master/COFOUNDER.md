# Co-Founder Search: Business/Operations Lead

## Position Overview

Dyson Labs is seeking a co-founder with business development, government contracting, and operational expertise to complement our technical foundation. This is a full-time operational role focused on business development, fundraising, and government contracting.

**Company Stage:** Pre-seed / Pre-revenue
**Technology:** SCRAP (Secure Capabilities and Routed Authorization Protocol) - TRL 2-3
**Target Markets:** DoD, NASA, commercial satellite operators
**Equity:** Open to negotiation based on contribution and fit

---

## Critical Requirement: Service-Disabled Veteran Status

**SDVOSB (Service-Disabled Veteran-Owned Small Business) certification is a strategic priority.**

### Why This Matters

| Benefit | Value |
|---------|-------|
| Federal set-asides | 5% federal goal (~$30B/year target market) |
| Sole source authority | Direct awards up to $5M without competition |
| DoD preference | 39% of all SDVOSB contracts are DoD |
| Micro-purchase preference | $10K-$250K direct procurement |

### Eligibility Requirements

- Service-connected disability rating (any percentage)
- Unconditional discharge (honorable, general, etc.)
- US citizen
- ≥51% ownership/control of the company

**Action:** VetCert application through SBA (60-90 day processing, free)

## Interview Process

### Stage 1: Initial Screening (30 min)

**Goal:** Verify basic fit and mutual interest

Questions:
1. Tell me about your military service and transition to civilian career
2. What's your experience with government contracting?
3. Why are you interested in space technology / satellite communications?
4. What's your availability and location?

### Stage 2: Deep Dive (60-90 min)

**Goal:** Assess domain expertise and operational capability

#### Government Contracting Experience

- [ ] Walk me through an SBIR proposal you've written or contributed to. What was the outcome?
- [ ] How do you approach building relationships with government program managers?
- [ ] What's your experience with FAR/DFAR compliance requirements?
- [ ] Have you worked with OTA (Other Transaction Authority) contracts? How do they differ from traditional contracts?
- [ ] Describe a time you helped a company win a government contract. What was your role?

#### Business Development

- [ ] How do you approach customer discovery in the government space?
- [ ] What's your process for identifying and qualifying opportunities?
- [ ] Tell me about a partnership you developed. What made it successful?
- [ ] How do you balance pursuing multiple funding sources (grants vs. contracts vs. investment)?

#### Operational Leadership

- [ ] Describe your experience in early-stage companies. What was the hardest part?
- [ ] How do you prioritize when everything seems urgent?
- [ ] What operational systems would you put in place first at a pre-seed company?
- [ ] How do you handle ambiguity and incomplete information?

#### Strategic Thinking

- [ ] Based on our presentations, what do you see as the biggest market opportunity?
- [ ] What concerns or risks would you want to address first?
- [ ] How would you approach the SDVOSB certification process?
- [ ] What's your 90-day plan if you joined?

### Stage 3: Working Session (2-3 hours)

**Goal:** Evaluate collaboration style and problem-solving

Activities:
1. **Review a real opportunity**: Analyze an upcoming SBIR topic together
2. **Draft an outline**: Sketch the approach for a proposal or customer pitch
3. **Discuss equity/terms**: Ensure alignment on expectations

### Stage 4: Reference Checks

- [ ] Previous co-founders or business partners
- [ ] Government contracting colleagues
- [ ] Military service references (character, not just performance)

## Red Flags to Watch For

| Red Flag | Why It Matters |
|----------|---------------|
| No hands-on SBIR/STTR experience | Learning curve too steep for immediate contribution |
| Unwilling to do operational work | Early-stage requires rolling up sleeves |
| Overly optimistic timelines | Government contracting is slow; realism is essential |
| No network in target market | Relationships are critical for BD |
| Wants to "advise" not "operate" | We need full-time operational leadership |
| Unclear about veteran status | SDVOSB eligibility is non-negotiable |

## Equity Framework

Equity allocation depends on:

1. **Contribution scope**: Full-time operational vs. advisory

3. **Capital contribution**: Cash investment, if any
4. **SDVOSB value**: Certification enables significant contract access

- What ownership percentage do you expect?
- Are you able to make a capital contribution?
- What's your timeline for full-time commitment?
- How do you think about founder equity in early-stage companies?
